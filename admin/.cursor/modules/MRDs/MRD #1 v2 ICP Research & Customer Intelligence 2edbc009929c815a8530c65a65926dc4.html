Executive Summary

Following the January 14, 2026 bi-weekly sprint call, FFC leadership made a strategic decision to pause product packaging experimentation and focus on foundational customer research and ICP definition. This pivot recognizes that testing offer variants and segmentation strategies is premature when the fundamental question of "who are we serving?" remains unclear.

This MRD outlines the research-first approach that will inform future product development, replacing the previous test-and-iterate segmentation strategy with a learn-then-build methodology.

Critical insight from leadership: "We need to understand retention data, get the P&L documentation, and have clear ICP definitions before deeper module work can be meaningful."

Problem Statement

What Changed?

The original MRD #1 (Product Packaging & Segmentation Testing) assumed:

  • ‚úÖ ICP definitions existed and were validated
  • ‚úÖ Cohort insights would inform offer testing
  • ‚úÖ The current product model had proven retention patterns

The January 14 call revealed these assumptions are false:

  • ‚ùå Tent House founders observed are "really different" from expected ICP
  • ‚ùå Cohort Builder (MRD #4) is on hold until new product exists
  • ‚ùå Missing critical data: retention assumptions, P&L per offering, discount strategy
  • ‚ùå 2% conversion rate suggests fundamental product-market fit questions

Why This Matters

You cannot optimize what you don't understand. Testing pricing tiers, bundle configurations, and offer variants is expensive theater if the underlying product doesn't resonate with a clearly defined customer segment.

Alison's strategic clarity: Focus on ICP + new product development rather than automating current operations when the root product isn't clicking with customers.

Objectives

Primary Goal

Define validated ICPs and document customer insights that will inform new product development and go-to-market strategy.

Success Criteria

By end of 2-week sprint:

  1. ‚úÖ ICP Documentation: One-pager per target founder type with demographics, psychographics, business characteristics, pain points, and willingness-to-pay signals
  1. ‚úÖ Retention Analysis: Documented retention data with qualitative justification (not just projections) explaining why certain founders stay vs. churn (Retention goals/Benchmarks on where we need to go)

  1. ‚úÖ Customer Research Repository: Interview notes, survey data, and behavioral analysis accessible to full team

Strategic outcome: Team can confidently answer "Who are we building for?" and "What do they actually need?" before investing in product experimentation.

Approach & Methodology

Phase 1: Discovery (Days 1-3)

Customer Interview Recruitment:

  • Select founders from different cohorts (early, mid, recent)
  • Include both high-engagement and at-risk founders
  • Ensure representation across business stages and backgrounds

Data Gathering:

  • Pull retention data from existing cohorts
  • Compile purchase history and basket analysis
  • Review historical feedback and support tickets
  • Analyze behavioral data (engagement patterns, feature usage)

Phase 2: Research Execution (Days 4-8)

Qualitative Research:

  • Conduct 10-15 founder interviews (30-45 min each)
  • Focus on: motivations, pain points, decision criteria, product value perception, unmet needs
  • Document verbatim quotes for pattern identification

Quantitative Analysis:

  • Cohort retention analysis with statistical significance
  • Purchase behavior segmentation
  • Discount effectiveness measurement
  • Feature adoption tracking

Phase 3: Synthesis & Documentation (Days 9-12)

ICP Development:

  • Identify founder archetypes from interview patterns
  • Validate archetypes against behavioral data
  • Create detailed one-pagers per segment
  • Assign confidence levels based on sample size

Product Positioning:

  • Map offerings to validated segments
  • Identify product gaps and opportunities
  • Document pricing sensitivity insights
  • Create go-to-market recommendations

Phase 4: Delivery & Alignment (Days 13-14)

Stakeholder Review:

  • Present findings to FFC leadership
  • Validate ICP definitions with team's tacit knowledge
  • Align on priority segments for new product development
  • Get approval on next steps

Documentation:

  • Finalize all deliverables in shared workspace
  • Create accessible research repository
  • Establish process for ongoing customer intelligence

Risks & Mitigation

Risk 1: Low Interview Participation

Impact: Insufficient qualitative data for pattern identification

Likelihood: Medium

Mitigation:

  • Offer incentives for interview participation (e.g., extended access, credits)
  • Make scheduling flexible (15-min quick calls available)
  • Use existing relationships for warm introductions

Risk 2: Inconclusive Findings

Impact: Cannot define clear ICP segments, delaying next phase

Likelihood: Low-Medium

Mitigation:

  • Triangulate qualitative and quantitative data
  • Accept that "we serve multiple distinct segments" is a valid finding
  • Document confidence levels and areas requiring further research

Risk 3: Scope Creep into Execution

Impact: Sprint extends beyond research into building solutions

Likelihood: Medium

Mitigation:

  • Clear scope boundaries established in this MRD
  • Leadership explicitly separated "learn" phase from "build" phase
  • Hold execution work for subsequent sprints after ICP validation

Risk 4: January Cohort Demands Overwhelm Research Priority

Impact: Team gets pulled into cohort operations, delaying ICP work

Likelihood: Medium-High

Mitigation:

  • Chris Toy clarified: January cohort is "case by case" with Kalli leading
  • MH-1 provides spot help only, not full project management
  • Alison aware of resource reallocation need on FFC side
